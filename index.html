The Poems

William Shakespeare's Sonnets consists of 154 poems, each fourteen lines long (three quatrains followed by a rhyming couplet).

The perhaps most famous of them all

Sonnet 18 is perhaps the most famous, and begins like this:

Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?
Thou art more lovely and more temperate.
Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May,
And summer's lease hath all too short a date.

Shakespeare's life

Shakespeare's life was extremely funny even though he was born to respected parents. It soon became apparent that young William was a wordsmith manque. His father therefore sent him to a school for the linguistically gifted. There, little Bill was taught how to read and write.
